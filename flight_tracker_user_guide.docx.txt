How Does It Work? (Basic Idea)


1. The Antenna Listens
Airplanes constantly broadcast their position using something called ADS-B. It's a radio signal on a specific frequency (1090 MHz). A small antenna picks up these signals.
2. The Computer Decodes
A tiny computer (called a Raspberry Pi — it's about the size of a credit card) takes those radio signals and figures out what they mean. It extracts the flight number, position, speed, altitude, and more.
3. The Internet Adds Details
The computer connects to your home Wi-Fi and looks up extra information online: the airline name, where the flight is going, what kind of plane it is, and a photo of that specific aircraft.
4. The Display Shows Everything
All of this information gets displayed on the 7-inch screen in an easy-to-read format. You just plug it in, set your location once, and it does the rest automatically.

How Far Can It See?
The range depends on where you put the antenna:


Indoor Antenna (near a window):
* You'll typically see planes within 20-50 miles
* That's usually 10-30 planes at any given time, depending on your location
Outdoor Antenna (on the roof or high up):
* You can see planes 100-200+ miles away
* Could track 50-100+ planes at once if you're near busy airspace
* Requires running a cable outside (not hard, but takes a bit more work)


The signals travel in straight lines, so higher placement = better reception. Even indoors near a window works great.


How Hard Is It to Set Up?
The setup is designed to be as simple as possible. Here's what you'll need to do:


One-Time Setup (About 10-15 Minutes):
Step 1: Hardware Assembly
* Plug the screen into the raspberry pi with the HDMI cable
* Plug the radio receiver into the raspberry pi USB port
* Connect the antenna to the radio receiver
* Plug in the power supply
* Connect the four buttons (they're pre-wired in the case)
Step 2: Software Setup
* Turn it on — it creates a temporary Wi-Fi network
* Connect to that network with your phone or laptop
* A setup page opens automatically in your web browser
* Click on a map to set your location (or type in your address)
* Enter your home Wi-Fi password so it can connect to the internet
* Click Save
Step 3: Done!
* The system restarts and connects to your Wi-Fi
* Within 30-60 seconds, you'll start seeing planes on the screen
* Mount it on the wall


After this initial setup, you never have to configure anything again.1

Day-to-Day Use
Once it's set up, using it is super simple:


Normal Operation:

Using the Buttons:

Looking at History:
